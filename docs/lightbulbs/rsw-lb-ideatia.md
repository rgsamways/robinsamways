## The gap

Robin has purchased the domain `ideatia.ca` with the intent to build an idea incubator — a platform for helping people develop and validate ideas. He's already flagged, before any scoping has happened, that this space is almost certainly crowded: idea-incubation and startup-validation platforms are a well-trodden category. Without a specific, differentiated niche, Ideatia risks being a generic "idea board" with no reason for anyone to choose it over an existing option.

## The idea

Ideatia: a portfolio project built around a specific, carved-out niche within idea incubation — not yet identified. The domain is secured; the concept and its differentiation are the open work. Robin's own framing was explicit: "I'm certain there are many, so we'll have to carve out a niche that no one is doing yet" — meaning the very first step here is competitive-landscape research to find that gap, not building anything yet.

## Why it matters beyond convenience

- A real product idea with a domain already committed to it — more concrete than a purely speculative lightbulb, and worth tracking as its own thing rather than letting it drift.
- Explicitly stated to need a differentiated niche before any build decision — a good discipline to hold to rather than starting to build a generic version and hoping a niche emerges later.
- A real, dated example of Ideatia's own incubation process already happened tonight, before any software exists — see `rsw-lb-small-trusted-network-thesis`. That's worth more than it sounds: it's proof the *process* (rough curiosity → research → refined insight) is real and repeatable, independent of whatever specific niche Ideatia eventually picks.

## Open questions

- What's the actual niche? Nothing is decided yet — this is the single open question that has to resolve before anything else about this project makes sense.
- Is this meant to sit inside the Portfolio showcase (a one-off "look what I built" piece), or does it have "graduated" ambitions like Farpost/Sreditor (a real product Robin intends to develop further)? Too early to say without a niche.
- Does `rsw-lb-trademark-ai-assistant` end up as its own standalone project? Confirmed *not* to be Ideatia's niche itself — that framing was corrected once Robin clarified the small-trusted-network thesis is a process example, not a niche candidate.
