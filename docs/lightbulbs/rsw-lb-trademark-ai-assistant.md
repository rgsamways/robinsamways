## The gap

Robin asked what AI-assisted applications currently exist for investigating/registering trademarks and connecting users with human "brokers" (trademark attorneys/advisors) for availability and legal judgment calls. A quick live search (2026-07-08) turned up a real, current landscape worth recording before it's forgotten:

- **Trademark Engine** launched an AI-powered ChatGPT app (June 2026) integrated with its own registration platform — trademark search, application preparation, and USPTO filing assistance, adjusting guidance based on business type/strategy.
- **USPTO itself** has shipped AI tooling directly: a beta AI-driven image search (April 2026) for finding visually similar registered marks, and **Class ACT** (Trademark Classification Agentic Codification Tool, launched March 2026) — an AI agent that handles behind-the-scenes application processing before a human examiner sees it.
- The general trend in this space is AI-assisted **DIY search-and-file tools**, and separately, **the USPTO's own internal AI-assisted examination tooling**. Nothing found in this quick pass was clearly positioned as a **marketplace-style matching service** — an AI intake/triage layer that hands off specifically to a human broker/attorney once the case needs real legal judgment, rather than trying to fully automate the filing itself.

## The idea

Not yet a defined product — this started as a research question, not a pitch. But the gap the research surfaced is real: most existing tools either do the DIY filing themselves (Trademark Engine) or improve the examiner's side of the process (USPTO's own tools). The **"AI does the initial investigation, then connects you to the right human broker for the parts that need judgment"** framing doesn't appear to be a crowded lane based on this pass — worth a deeper look if this becomes a real candidate project.
